Pricing Strategy (Important for a New Gallery)

Collectors often look for confidence and consistency in pricing.

Emerging Artists
•	$800 – $3,500 range for paintings 
•	$500 – $2,500 for works on paper
•	Small sculptures $1,000 – $4,000

Mid-Career Artists	
•	$3,500 – $12,000+ depending on scale and recognition

Limited Works / Special Pieces.        
•	Larger or signature works priced higher to establish value.

Standard Gallery Commission
•	50/50 split between gallery and artist (industry norm).

Important Pricing Rules	
•	Prices should never be cheaper outside the gallery.	Artists should increase prices slowly and consistently as work sells.
•	Maintain clear size-based pricing so collectors understand value. Example structure for paintings:
•	24x24: $1,800
•	30x40: $3,200
•	48x60: $6,500 Consistency builds collector trust.

1. Showing Too Many Artists

New galleries often try to represent 20–30 artists right away. This spreads attention thin and confuses collectors.

Better approach:
Start with 5–8 strong artists whose work feels cohesive.

Collectors should feel like the gallery has a clear taste and vision, not just a random mix of art.


2. Hanging Too Much Art

Tourist galleries often cover every wall with artwork.

Serious galleries show fewer works with more space around them.

Benefits:
each piece feels more important
collectors can focus
the gallery feels more museum-like Many high-end galleries only show 8–15 works at a time.


3. Inconsistent Pricing

Collectors quickly lose confidence if they see:
similar paintings at wildly different prices
artists selling cheaper work directly online
sudden price jumpsA clear size-based pricing structure solves this problem.Example:
24x24 – $1,800
36x48 – $4,200
60x60 – $9,000 Consistency builds trust.
